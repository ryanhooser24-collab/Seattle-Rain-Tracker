Sizing & limits
Max per fill ?
Hard dollar cap on each individual order placement. Each time the execution loop fills at a price level, this is the ceiling — regardless of Kelly output or book depth. Start small while testing, scale up as win rate confirms the model.
 $25
Max per ticker ?
Total dollar cap across all fills on a single ticker per session. The loop walks the book while grade holds A — this is the hard stop. Defaults to 3× max per fill.
 $75
Kelly multiplier ?
Scales the Kelly formula output. Kelly fraction = (p × b − q) / b, where p = model probability, q = 1−p, b = (1−ask)/ask. E.g. model 60%, ask 40¢ → b=1.5, Kelly=33%. At 0.5× multiplier, bet size = 16.5% of bankroll unit. Use 0.5× until 100+ trades confirm model calibration.
 0.50×
Bankroll unit ?
Dollar amount Kelly sizing is applied against. Set to 5–10% of your trading account. Kelly size = 0.5 × unit × Kelly fraction. Update as your account grows.

Your primary exposure control. Kalshi settlements lag until the next morning, so a daily loss limit would block good signals based on stale P&L. This is the real guardrail — max exposure = this × max per ticker. At defaults (5 × $75 = $375 max deployed at once).

Calibration columns explained
Base σ (RMSE) — how far off OM's full-month forecast typically is from the NWS CLM actual, in inches. Computed over 12 months of history. This is the uncertainty radius around any EOM projection. Smaller = more predictable city.
Bias — the mean error (OM forecast minus actual). Negative means OM systematically under-forecasts. The model adds this back to every projection silently before computing probabilities.
Direction — WET means OM over-forecasts (positive bias), DRY means it under-forecasts (negative bias). All 8 cities are currently DRY — OM tends to underestimate monthly totals.

Workflow — ⚡ Recalibrate All is the only button you need to run, once after every deploy. Everything else is automatic.
